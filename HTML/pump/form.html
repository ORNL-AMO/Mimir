Pump Type: represents what style of pump is being used based off of the listings in the standard ANSI/HI 1.3-2000.

Pump Speed: The operating or nameplate speed at which the pump is running at.

Drive: This drop-down selection menu allows the user to define whether the pump is direct driven by the motor or belt-driven. There are three options for belt drives: V-Belt, Notched V-Belt and Synchronous Belt Drives. Of the three, V-Belt drives are the least efficient, Notched V-Belts are slightly better, and Synchronous Belts are the most efficient belts.

Fluid Type: the type of fluid in your system.

Fluid Temperature: the average temperature of the fluid in your system.

Specific Gravity: the ratio of the density of the fluid to water at standard condition.

Kinematic viscosity: the ratio of absolute (or dynamic) viscosity to density - a quantity in which no force is involved.
This can be obtained by dividing the absolute viscosity of a fluid with the fluid mass density. It is expressed here in terms of CentiStokes.
This value is automatically calculated for the chosen fluid at the specified temperature. For all fluids other than water, this value is only valid at the reference temperature, usually 25 deg C (68 deg F). It can also be edited manually.

Stages: The number of pump stages.

Pump Type

Line Frequency: the main supply frequency. In North America, the standard frequency is 60 HZ. Elsewhere, 50 HZ is often the standard.

Rated Motor Power: represents the rated power for the motor.

Motor RPM: the nameplate speed of the motor

Efficiency Class: the classification of efficiency given to motor. This can be found either on the nameplate or based on how the motor compares with the NEMA MG 1-2003.

Rated Voltage: This voltage is the motor design (nameplate) voltage
